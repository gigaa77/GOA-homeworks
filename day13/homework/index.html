MOTORCYCLES

A motorcycle is a two-wheeled motor vehicle powered by an internal combustion engine or, increasingly, by electricity.

They're designed for speed, maneuverability, and efficiency — perfect for commuting, sport, touring, or adventure.

    1. The History of Motorcycles

  • The first motorcycle dates back to the late 19th century.
  • 1885: Gottlieb Daimler and Wilhelm Maybach created the Reitwagen (“riding car”) — the first gas-powered two-wheeler.
  • By the early 1900s, companies like Harley-Davidson, Indian, and Triumph were mass-producing motorcycles.
