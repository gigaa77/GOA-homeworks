Animals

Dogs

Dogs are one of the oldest companions of humans. Their relationship with us goes back at least 15,000-30,000 years, when ancient wolves began living near humans for food and shelter.

Over thousands of years, humans selected wolves with calmer, friendlier behavior — and slowly, wolves evolved into the dogs we know today.

There are more than 350+ dog breeds worldwide, each created for a specific purpose:

  1. Working dogs
    • Rottweiler
    • Doberman
    • German Shepherd
  • Hunting dogs
    • Pointer
    • Beagle
    • Spaniel

Cats

Cats are one of the most unique animals humans live with. Unlike dogs, cats were never fully “trained” or molded by humans — they kept their independent nature.

They started living with people thousands of years ago mostly because they hunted mice around farms. Humans liked them, cats liked the free food, and slowly they became our companions.

Unusual Animals:

Axolotl

The axolotl is a type of salamander that remains in its larval stage for its entire life. Unlike most amphibians, it never fully develops into a land-dwelling adult and lives entirely in water.1



Tardigrade (Water Bear)

Tardigrades are tiny, microscopic creatures, usually 0.3-0.5 mm long, found in moss, lichen, soil, and freshwater around the world. Despite their size, they are extremely hardy and almost indestructible, earning them the nickname “water bears.”
